David Deutsch has a **fantastic** article called **Creative Blocks:** *The very laws of physics imply that artificial intelligence must be possible. What's holding us up?* [[link]](http://aeon.co/magazine/technology/david-deutsch-artificial-intelligence/). I can't recommend it any higher. In it, he outlines what he thinks the major problems are in AI research, and how fundamentally we're thinking about in the wrong way. He argues for a core philosophical restructuing, centered around the ability to be *creative*. I am not (and cannot be) doing it justice. Just please, please read it.

**Read it.**
