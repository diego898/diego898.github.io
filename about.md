I'm currently an [Academic Pathways](https://gradschool.vanderbilt.edu/postdoctoral/academic-pathways/) Postdoctoral Fellow at [Vanderbilt University](http://www.vanderbilt.edu/), jointly hosted in the [Department of Biomedical Informatics](https://medschool.vanderbilt.edu/dbmi/) in the [Computational Medicine Lab](https://github.com/ComputationalMedicineLab/egpmrp) by [Dr. Thomas Lasko](https://medschool.vanderbilt.edu/dbmi/person/thomas-lasko-md-phd) and in the [Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science](https://engineering.vanderbilt.edu/eecs/) in the [Computational Economics Research Lab (CERL)](https://my.vanderbilt.edu/cerl/) by [Dr. Eugene Vorobeychik](http://vorobeychik.com/). I love working in and learning about information theory, statistics, probability, optimization and neural modeling.

I received my PhD in [Bioengineering](http://be.ucsd.edu/) at the
[University of California: San Diego](http://ucsd.edu/) in the
[Neural Interaction Lab](coleman.ucsd.edu/) where I was working on developing theoretical and numerical techniques to make *efficient* use of *information*, with special applications to global health. 

Before becoming a graduate student at UCSD I was an undergrad in
[Computer Engineering](http://www.eng.ufl.edu/) at the [University of Florida](http://www.ufl.edu/). While there, I worked in the Advanced Computing and Information Systems [(ACIS)](https://www.acis.ufl.edu/) Lab developing the infrastructure necessary to enable Brain Machine Interface research.

I believe in [keeping your identity small](http://www.paulgraham.com/identity.html), [writing as an aid to thinking](http://www.paulgraham.com/essay.html), and [writing as you speak](http://www.paulgraham.com/talk.html). I believe in [slowing down](https://lareviewofbooks.org/article/in-praise-of-slowness/), and that a thing worth doing, is [worth doing badly](http://www.chesterton.org/a-thing-worth-doing/). I acknowledge the pervasiveness of [research debt](http://distill.pub/2017/research-debt/), and believe in the invaluable role of [research distillers](http://distill.pub/2017/research-debt/).
